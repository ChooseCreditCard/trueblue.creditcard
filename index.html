JetBlue TrueBlue: Points, Mosaic Status & Credit Cards

TrueBlue is the loyalty program of JetBlue Airways. This guide explains how points are earned, how Mosaic status works, what you can redeem points for, and how credit cards can significantly increase your earning rate.

Best Cards for Earning TrueBlue Points

1. What the TrueBlue Program Is

TrueBlue is JetBlue’s points-based loyalty program, designed around simplicity and revenue-based redemptions. There are no award charts — the number of points needed generally aligns with the cash fare.

  • Points – earned on flights, partners and credit cards.
  • Mosaic status – JetBlue’s elite tier with priority perks.
  • Family pooling – a standout feature allowing households to combine points.
  • Partner earning – including American Airlines under the NEA (market dependent).

2. How TrueBlue Points Are Earned

You earn points from:

  • JetBlue flights – points based on the fare and elite bonuses.
  • American Airlines flights (in supported regions) – reciprocal earning.
  • Co-branded credit cards – accelerated earning on everyday purchases.
  • Hotels, car rentals and shopping – partner activity.
  • Family pooling – combine points with up to 7 people.

Because JetBlue is primarily US-based, most TrueBlue members build balances through credit card spend.

3. How Credit Cards Boost TrueBlue Earnings

JetBlue-linked credit cards often provide:

  • Bonus earn rates on dining, groceries or JetBlue purchases.
  • Large welcome bonuses for new cardholders.
  • Mosaic-qualifying spend (on select cards).
  • Travel perks like free checked bags or priority boarding.

For many travelers, credit cards are the fastest route to both points and Mosaic-qualifying metrics.

4. Redemption Categories in TrueBlue

TrueBlue points can be redeemed for:

  • JetBlue flights – dynamic pricing tied closely to cash fares.
  • Mint business class – one of the most aspirational uses of TrueBlue points.
  • Partner flights (market dependent).
  • JetBlue Vacations – packages in some regions.
  • Other travel redemptions via partners.

JetBlue does not use award charts, making the system transparent but also tied directly to ticket prices.

5. JetBlue Mosaic Elite Status

Mosaic is the TrueBlue elite tier, offering priority services and valuable in-flight benefits. Qualification rules depend on a combination of flight activity and credit card spend on eligible cards.

  • Mosaic 1 – base tier with priority perks.
  • Mosaic 2 – enhanced benefits.
  • Mosaic 3 & 4 – higher recognition, extra Mint upgrade certificates, and partner perks.
